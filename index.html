Why Contribute?

We study robot navigation using high-fidelity 3D reconstructions. By sharing your captures of an indoor scene (a classroom, a lounge, a conference room) on campus, you’ll help us improve training environments and advance embodied AI research.

All submissions are stored securely in our Georgia Tech research drive and will be used strictly for academic purposes in accordance with our IRB protocol.

Contributors will receive $x per approved scan (up to $y total).

Requirements & Tips for Great Captures

  • Device Requirement: Use an iPhone Pro or iPad Pro with LiDAR (e.g., iPhone 12 Pro or newer).
  • What to Scan: Focus on indoor scenes like classrooms, lounges, and conference rooms. One typical scan should take 10-20 minutes.
  • Privacy First: Avoid including any people or personally identifiable information (PII) in your scans.
  • Lighting: Capture in good, even lighting to reduce noise and improve mesh quality.
  • Coverage: Walk slowly around the entire space; wait until the purple shadow on your scan clears to move.

Following these guidelines helps us create cleaner, more usable datasets for EmbodiedSplat and ensures your contribution is valuable for research.

How to Capture & Upload

1. Setup Polycam

Use the Polycam app to scan an indoor scene. Go to Settings -> Developer Mode and turn Developer Mode on.

2. Scan

Capture data in Space mode. This would use the lidar sensor.

3. Process

After scanning, tap Process to process the data in the Polycam app.

4. Export

Click the download icon in the top right corner.

5. Extract GLTF file

Select GLTF to export to a .glb file. Upload this file.

6. Extract raw data

Select raw data to export a .zip file. Upload this file as well.
